About

Introduction

Formula 1® racing began in 1950 and is the world’s most prestigious motor racing competition, as well as the world’s most popular annual sporting series. The 203 FIA Formula One World Championship™ runs from March to December. As of the 2022 São Paulo Grand Prix, 1,078 World Championship events have been held in 34 countries at 76 racing circuits. Formula One World Championship Limited is part of Formula 1® and holds the exclusive commercial rights to the FIA Formula One World Championship™. The objective of a Formula 1 contest is to determine the winner of a race. The driver who crosses the finish line first after completing a pre-determined number of laps is declared the winner.

Rules, Teams

The name ‘Formula’ comes from the set of rules that the participating cars and drivers must follow. The rules apply to all three components of an F1 car: chassis, engine and tires. A Formula One car is an open-wheel, open-cockpit, single-seat racing car for the purpose of being used in Formula One competitions. The F1 races are conducted on specifically built racing tracks, sometimes they are conducted on closed public roads as well.

F1 team (total of 10 teams currently) can have maximum of 4 drivers per season. There is support staff with every F1 team that plays a vital role in the team’s success.

Pit Stops, Grand Prix

It's incredibly important to choose the right strategy for the race. Drivers require at least one stop per race to change the tires, sometimes to a different type. Soft provide stronger grip and allow faster cornering while hards take much longer to wear out at the cost of less grip. Timely pit stops are crucial for overtaking the opponents.

A Grand Prix (GP) weekend consists of 3 free practices, a qualifying session where drivers' lap times determine their starting grid position and, of course, the main race.
