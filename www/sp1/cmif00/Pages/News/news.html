Bruno scores two as Portugal progress

Manchester United midfielder Bruno Fernandes scored both goals for Portugal as they beat Uruguay 2-0 to secure their qualification to the last 16 of the 2022 World Cup. Our no.8 was in superb form as he netted his match-winning brace, firstly opening the scoring after 54 minutes, when his looping cross narrowly evaded the head of former Reds team-mate Cristiano Ronaldo to find the back of the net. With the game teetering on a knife edge in injury time, Fernandes forced a penalty when he superbly knocked the ball through the legs of Jose Maria Gimenez and the Atletico Madrid man handled it as he fell to the ground. After a VAR check, Alireza Faghani awarded a spot-kick and Bruno confidently stepped up to take it himself, firing past Uruguay goalkeeper Sergio Rochet and ensuring Portugal's progression in the competition.

Reds star in Brazil win

Manchester United’s Antony, Casemiro and Fred featured for Brazil as they kicked off their 2022 World Cup campaign with victory over Serbia. The five-time winners of the competition triumphed 2-0 at the Lusail Stadium in Qatar, ensuring that they put an early three points on the board in Group G. Tottenham forward Richarlison got both of the difference-making strikes in the second half, with the latter - an acrobatic effort - an instant Goal of the Tournament contender. Midfielfer Casemiro was the only one in the starting lineup, however Fred and Anthony were both able to make their apperances later in the game.

Ronaldo is leaving

Cristiano Ronaldo is to leave Manchester United by mutual agreement, with immediate effect. The club thanks him for his immense contribution across two spells at Old Trafford, scoring 145 goals in 346 appearances, and wishes him and his family well for the future. Everyone at Manchester United remains focused on continuing progress under Erik ten Hag and working together to deliver success on the pitch.

Garnacho wins November Goal of the Month

Manchester United forward Alejandro Garnacho is the winner of our November 2022 Goal of the Month prize. Your votes see Alejandro cap off a memorable month, in which he also won the club’s Player of the Month accolade. The 18-year-old’s last-gasp winner away to Fulham, in the men’s team’s final game before the World Cup break, topped the official poll with 55 per cent of the vote.

Victor Lindelof on a mission with UNICEF

Manchester United centre-back has been using the winter break to work alongside UNICEF, travelling to The Gambia with the charitable organisation. The defender arrived in the West African country with his wife Maja last week and has since been to schools and hospitals to learn about the issues facing the people living there. During his trip, Victor has been keeping a thread on his Twitter account, documenting the amazing things that he has been doing, the new information that he is taking in, and the way it makes him feel.
